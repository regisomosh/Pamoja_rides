Frequently Asked Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Carpooling, also known as car-sharing or ridesharing, is the sharing of car journeys so that more than one person travels in a car, and prevents the need for others to have to drive to a location themselves.

Carpooling typically involves a group of people agreeing to share a car ride together to a common destination. They may take turns driving or contribute to the cost of gas and other expenses.

Carpooling helps reduce traffic congestion, lowers fuel costs, reduces carbon emissions, and promotes social interaction among commuters. It also reduces the need for parking spaces and helps save time by using high occupancy vehicle (HOV) lanes.
